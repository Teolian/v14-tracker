The shaded band is the forecast’s promise: 8 of 10 hours should land inside it. Narrow band = confident day, wide = volatile day — that width is exactly the uncertainty a market player should price. Red dots = the hours the band missed.
How to read. Red dots on the black line mark measured hours that fell outside the shown band. The green line is the V14 forecast issued before the day began (time in the header, git-committed); the shaded band is its P10–P90 uncertainty interval (80% of reality should fall inside); the black line is what physical JMA pyranometers actually measured; the dashed blue line is the official JMA MSM forecast — the standard reference in the Japanese market.
